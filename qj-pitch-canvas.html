💬 Qualifying Sales Script

Pre-Call Mantra

Read this before every call. Get your head right before you dial.

  • Every call is an opportunity to grow and create relationships.
  • Every NO I receive is 1 step closer to a YES.
  • There is an abundance of work out there for my business, ready to be swept up.
  • This is not a transaction — this is about building relationship & trust
🕐 Target call duration: 8 to 15 minutes
Step 1 — Introduction

Keep it warm, natural, and confident.

"Hey! It's [YOUR NAME] from [COMPANY NAME]."

"Just giving you a call from your enquiry you gave online through our Facebook ads."

"I'm the owner of [Company Name] & thought I'd introduce myself."

"How are you today?"
Step 2 — Pre-Frame

Set the intention. Calm them down. Make them feel in control.

"Awesome! So on this call:"

"I'd love to know exactly what you're after so we can see if we're a good fit for each other."

"There's no pressure to move forward — this is just a quick chat to understand your project."

"Does that sound fair?"
Step 3 — Qualify the Lead

Understand their project, timeline, and budget.

"So tell me, what's the project you're looking at?"

"What's the reason behind the renovation — is it for yourself or are you planning to sell?"

"Have you had any other quotes done yet?"

"What stage are you at — just exploring or ready to move forward?"

"Do you have a budget in mind for this project?"

"Is there anything that would stop you from moving forward if we were a great fit?"
  • If under budget → explain value, reframe scope
  • If not ready → nurture sequence
  • If disqualified → politely end call
Step 4 — Build Rapport
"How long have you been in the home?"

"What made you decide now was the right time?"

"That's a great project — we've done a lot of [bathroom/kitchen] work in [area]."
Step 5 — Price Expectation
"Just so you know what to expect — I want to be upfront with you."

"Our average project like yours comes in around [AVERAGE PRICE]."

If too expensive → go back to under budget process.
If reasonable → move to Step 6.
Step 6 — Book the Appointment

This is the goal. Lock it in on the call.

  • Get a confirmed address
  • Find a time — book ALL decision makers
  • Lock it in while on the phone
  • Ask if finance is ready or if they need options
  • Find urgency — this is key
  • If price is at forefront, identify early
Long-Term Follow-Up Notes
  • Colorbond roofing — 30-year warranty, suits current climate
  • Position as long-term investment, not just a cost
  • Follow up at 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 1 month
